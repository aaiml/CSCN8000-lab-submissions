Analyzing the Choices:

  1. Decision Tree Bagging: The Decision Tree model benefits significantly from bagging, resulting in a substantial accuracy improvement from 0.79 (standalone) to 0.86 (with bagging). This highlights the potential of bagging to alleviate the limitations of a standalone Decision Tree.

  2. SVM Bagging: On the other hand, both standalone SVM and SVM with bagging consistently achieve higher accuracy than the Decision Tree models. This suggests that SVM is intrinsically better at handling the dataset.

Choosing the Best Model:
While the Decision Tree model experiences a substantial improvement with bagging, it's important to consider the absolute accuracy values achieved by both SVM and Decision Tree models:

  • SVM with Bagging: 0.88 accuracy (cross-validation)
  • Decision Tree with Bagging: 0.85 accuracy (cross-validation)

  2. Consistency: The SVM model consistently outperforms the Decision Tree model across all scenarios, including standalone and bagging.

Decision-Making:
Given that the SVM model not only has a higher accuracy in both standalone and bagging scenarios but also maintains consistency across different settings, it remains the more preferable choice for this specific classification task based on the accuracy metric.
